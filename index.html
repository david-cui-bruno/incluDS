What is Down syndrome?

Down syndrome is a natural difference that starts before birth. It happens when a person has an extra copy of chromosome 21. Chromosomes are tiny â€œinstruction booksâ€ in our cells. Most people have two copies of chromosome 21. People with Down syndrome have three. You may hear three types: trisomy 21 (most common), translocation, and mosaic.

People with Down syndrome are part of every community. Many children and adults with Down syndrome learn in school, work at jobs, build friendships, and enjoy hobbies. Inclusion means everyone belongs and has a voice.

Quick facts (friendly and positive)

  • About 1 in 700 babies in the United States is born with Down syndrome.
  • Down syndrome is found in families of every background.
  • Early services like speech, physical therapy, and occupational therapy help children build daily skills.
  • Regular checkups for vision, hearing, sleep, thyroid, heart, and dental health help you stay well.
